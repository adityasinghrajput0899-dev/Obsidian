## **1. Introduction to Titles**

The product title or product name (the **Item name** field on the **List your products** page) is the first thing that customers see when they search for a product on Amazon. Titles must clearly and concisely describe key product information. Clear titles help customers to identify, differentiate, and compare products, making it easier for them to make decisions and increasing the likelihood of a sale. *[Source: amazon seller central]*

Existing product titles have become increasingly long and noisy as sellers directly optimize for search and discoverability through keyword stuffing thereby reducing readability, trust, and clarity. In **2026**, ASCS will increase the coverage of high-quality modular titles in the catalog from 17% to 90% non-media GVs WW, using Starfish (Catalog Generative AI). Modular titles solve the challenge of creating product titles that are both concise and informative through its structure. Modular titles solve the challenge of creating concise yet readable titles that convey accurate product information, reduce buyer cognitive load, and enable customer experience, thereby establishes the foundation for the North Star vision for titles. Proven impact includes +$377M OPS and +$503M CSales across 85 product types, with an estimated incremental ~$2B OPS opportunity at scale through improved discoverability, relevance, and conversion. GCO will continue evaluating generated titles and provide insights to improve the precision of modular title generation and evaluation across **13 countries and 10 languages,** including English-language marketplaces (United States, United Kingdom, and Canada), the Japanese marketplace (Japan), and European-language marketplaces (France, Germany, Spain, Italy, Netherlands, Sweden, Poland and Belgium). ## 1.1  **What  is  a  Modular Title? **

A modular title is a two-part product naming structure that separates core product identity from differentiating attributes to ensure clarity, consistency, and Amazon compliance. *[Source: CSS Doc]*

**Product Identity (item_name aka identifier or title_identification)**

- The core product information; includes brand name, product type, and key product identifier
- Length must not exceed 75 characters (including spaces)
- No  performance claims or promotional language

*Example:* "Samsung Galaxy S24"

 

**Item Highlights (title_differentiation aka differentiator)**

- Key distinguishing features; includes relevant descriptive details: size, color, material, capacity, storage, or functional traits and more
- Maximum  up to 6 item highlights** **(differentiating details)
- Each  attribute must be 20 characters or less
- No  promotional language

*Example:* "128GB”, “Blue”, “5G”, “Unlocked”

 

**Purpose of Modular Title Structure**

The modular title’s two-part format creates a clean, scannable, describing differentiating features and relevant details, where:

- Product  identity remains simple and stable
- Attributes  stay structured, factual, and easy to maintain
- Customers  can quickly scan and compare products

**Example 1.1.1:**

**Original (non-modular) Title:** 

"MSI Thin GF63 15.6" 144Hz Gaming Laptop: 12th Gen Intel Core i7, NVIDIA GeForce RTX 4050, 16GB DDR4, 512GB NVMe SSD, Type-C, Cooler Boost 5, Win11 Home: Black 12VE-066US"

**Modular Title:** 

Thin GF63 15.6" Gaming Laptop, 16GB RAM, 512GB SSD, Black **(Identifier**)
144 Hz FHD,   Intel i7 Gen 12,   NVIDIA GeForce RTX 4050 (**Differentiator**)

### 1. 3  Product Title Requirements and Guidelines

Title requirements apply to all product types, except media product types, in all of the worldwide stores. These are **CONFIDENTIAL guidelines** for all sellers, vendors, and category teams listing products across Amazon's worldwide stores (23 countries). This documentation is pending publication and should be treated as internal-only information. *[Source: Seller Central]*

**SI. #**

**Requirement Type**

**Details**

1

**Character limit**

**Title Identification**: Titles must be **75 characters or fewer**, including spaces, and should include only essential product information - such as the **brand**, **product type or item name**, and **one to two key attributes**.

|  |  **Title Differentiation**: Item Highlights appear on the detail page below the Item Name. Can include a maximum of 6 item highlights, each <=20 characters, and should contain special or descriptive features differentiating the product from other products.|

2

**Promotional content**

Titles must not contain promotional phrases such as **“free shipping”** or **“100% quality guaranteed.”**

3

**Special characters**

Titles must not use the following special characters: **! , $ , ? , _ , { , } , ^ , ¬ , ¦**. Other special characters such as **~ , # , < , > , *** are allowed only in specific contexts (e.g., product identifiers like *“Style #4301”* or measurements like *“<10 lb”*). Decorative usage is not allowed (e.g., *“<< Size Kids XXS >>”*). ***However, exceptions exist. For example, PT GIFT_CARD requires price value with $ sign.***

4

**Content requirements**

Titles must include the **minimum necessary information** to clearly describe the product (e.g., *“Amazon Essentials Dress,” “Columbia Hiking Boots,” “Sony Headphones”*).

5

**Word repetition**

Titles must not contain the **same word more than twice**. Prepositions, articles, and conjunctions may be used more than twice. **Brand names are also subject to the two-instance limit.** If a word appears in a different context (e.g., *“Old Navy”* and *“Navy Blue”*), it is not considered a duplicate.

## 2. Objective of Audit Task

The Unstructured Attribute Quality Service (UAQS) is a guardrail that evaluates the quality of unstructured attributes, such as titles and bullet points, to prevent poor-quality data contributions from being integrated into an ASIN. The Title Quality Score (TQS) is an automated system that assesses title-specific quality metrics to ensure compliance. These guardrails maintain a precision threshold of ≥95%, with a recall rate of TK%.

**Goal**: Enable improvement of Starfish's precision to  **≥98%** for regeneration and evaluation of unstructured attributes through HITL audits

The following five checks are automated and are excluded from GCO audits:

- Identifier length (≤75 characters, including spaces)
- Brand inclusion
- Product Type or Item name presence
- Total differentiators count (≤6)
- Each differentiator (≤20 characters, including spaces)

To ensure the generation of high-quality modular titles, additional validation checks are required to verify compliance and content quality standards. We will perform audits comprising thirteen (13) distinct checks, organized into two segments: *six Compliance Checks* and *seven Content Quality Checks*. The '***Modular Title Quality Audit***' task evaluates Starfish-generated modular titles to ensure they meet the highest quality standards for Amazon customers. Modular titles are generated from three primary sources- **original title, bullet points, and product description**- all of which are readily accessible in the user interface for audit purposes. This SOP provides auditors with clear, structured instructions for validating Starfish-generated modular titles. Auditors assess content quality and verify that the generated modular titles comply with the guardrails outlined below. Through these validation checks, auditors identify issues and document their observations systematically.

#### **Compliance Checks**

**Compliance checks **validate that Starfish-generated modular titles comply to defined guardrails and are free from the following significant issues:

- Hallucination present
- Information drop
- Inconsistency present
- Redundancy present
- Hanging words 
- Restricted phrases 

#### **Content Quality Checks**

**Content quality checks** assess clarity, accuracy, structure, and completeness by evaluating presence of the following:

- Missing context
- Exact numeric specifications 
- Content misplacement
- Normalization and standardized terminologies
- Linguistic accuracy 
- Completeness gap
- Keyword stuffing

1

**Hallucination Present?**

**Hallucination** means Starfish model is adding words, terms, or details to the **modular title** that were **not present in the original title, bullet points or product description**. These invented additions are usually inferred by the model and can **misrepresent the product** and **mislead customers**. 
Update on **Hallucination Scenarios**
Specific scenarios are observed in the Starfish model where modular titles should be evaluated carefully for hallucination:
1.** Paraphrasing / Reformation **
This occurs when the model enhances the language, grammar, or phrasing of the modular title without changing the original meaning. This includes replacing words with synonymous or functionally equivalent expressions to improve clarity or readability. These are purely lexical improvements where the semantic meaning remains unchanged. Examples: 
- “with” → “compatible with”, “suitable for”, “fits”,  “included” 
- “without slip” → “anti-slip” (as a rewording of the same  meaning) 
**Decision:** Do **NOT **flag as hallucination
 
2.** Positive Descriptors**
This refers to cases where the model introduces additional information in the modular title that was not explicitly present in the original title, even if the added information appears reasonable or contextually relevant. The key distinction is the introduction of *new semantic content* that is not supported by the source. In these cases, the added detail is considered an enhancement in interpretation rather than an error. Examples: 
- Adding a product type (PT) name not present in the original title 
- Introducing descriptors such as “design,” “style,” “option,”  “pack,” or “size” or more when they are not explicitly stated in the source
**Decision:** Do **NOT **flag as hallucination in these cases

3. **Formatting improvements **are accepted under normalization (e.g., PU instead of Polyurethane or vis-e-versa)
**Decision:** Do **NOT **flag as hallucination

Flag **Issue Present**;** **Decision**:** "**Yes**" if the modular title includes any word, term, or detail explicitly not present in the original title, bullet points or product description resulting in fabricated (invented) information- features, attributes or descriptors.Confirm **No Issue**;** **Decision**: **"**No**" if the modular title uses the information present in the 3 sources with changes limited to reordering, standardizes or formatting.

**Reasoning**: Here "Clinically Proven" & "Treatment" are introduced in modular title which are probably inferred by the Starfish model. 'Clinically proven' is a false claim, and addition of treatment is also misleading which can cause customer distrust, especially for clinical conditions

2

**Information Drop?**

**Information drop** in a product title is the omission of key product specifications/features that were present in the original title but excluded in modular title. 

**What constitutes information drop?**
 - Variation attributes (represented by a letter, phrases, or characters representing product variations)
 - Specific values (color values, size specifications, materials, unit of measurments and its symbolic representation. eg., in., ", ®,™, *°*, : , x, & similar)
 - Brand names like "Only," "More," or "And" 
 - Context-dependent terms (e.g., "hand wash only" vs. "fitted sheet only")

**What is not information drop? ****Exceptions**
 - **Promotional language** ("great," "excellent," "and more")
 - **Attribute names** ("color" or "size" or "material" terms alone instead of values) e.g., color: blue; size: XL
 - **Synonymous expressions** conveying identical meaning (keyword stuffing) e.g., Curtain, Shades, Blinds
 - **Non-essential terms** adding no product value (generic details) e.g., Cheap prices, sexy, ultimate
 - **Grammar/connecting words** (prepositions, determiners, conjunctions, articles, superlative adjectives)
- **Availability options** (when drops multiple variating options that’s not specific data) e.g., sizes: 120*240 cms, 140*220 cms, 160*240 cms; eg., Black, White, Green, Red (Black)

***Please note that the information drop check applies only to existing ASINs. This validation will not be carried out for newly created (WPC) ASINs.***

Flag **Issue Present**;** **Decision**: **"**Yes**" if the modular title omits any key product specification or variation detail present in the original title (e.g., variation attributes, specific descriptors, units of measure/denotations) and the meaning is reduced or altered.Confirm **No Issue**; Decision**: **"**No**" if all key product information from the original title is retained, or if the removed content falls only under **defined exceptions** (promotional, generic, grammatical, or synonymous terms).

**Inconsistency Present?**

**Inconsistency** refers to conflicting, contradictory, or mismatched information within a modular title that occurs when:
a. **Identifiers versus differentiators conflict with each other**, creating internal contradictions within the title structure
b. **Modular title versus original title contradicts: **The modular title contradicts the original title when added details from bullet points or descriptions create mismatched product information. To verify accuracy a cross-referencing with bullet points and product descriptions is performed. ***This is applicable only when bullet points and product descriptions are present in the input section of the tool.***

Flag **Issue Present**;** **Decision**: **"**Yes**" if the modular title contains conflicting details within itself (**identifier vs. differentiator**) or contradicts the **original title** in product attributes or specifications.Confirm **No Issue**;** **Decision**: **"**No**" if all details in the modular title are internally consistent and fully aligned with the original title.

Example i
**Original Title:** Women's Smooth Cotton Non-Padded Strapless Underwire Minimizer Bra
**Identifier**: Strapless Minimizer Bra
**Differentiator**: ['Non-padded', 'Underwire', 'Full Cup', 'Seamless', 'Convertible Straps']
**Decision:** **Yes**
**Inconsistency Observations:** Strapless and Convertible Straps both are present in modular title
**Reasoning**: Original title has, "Strapless" but Modular title has both which are available in product description, thus constitutes for inconsistency 

Example i
**Original **Title: Zarbees Naturals Childrens Cough Syrup with Dark Honey Nighttime - Grape, **4 Fl. Ounces**
**Identifier**: Zarbees Naturals Childrens Cough Syrup with Dark Honey, Grape, **4 Oz**
**Differentiator**: ['Nighttime']
**Decision**: **Yes**
**Reasoning**: The unit representation is inconsistent “fluid ounces refers volume, whereas “Oz” denotes solid weight in ounces, leading to ambiguity. This inconsistency may change the meaning of the measurement and create confusion about the product’s actual quantity

4

**Redundancy Present?**

**Redundancy** occurs when words or phrases are triplicated within modular titles. A title exhibits redundancy when any term appears more than two (>2) repetitions. In other words, it occurs when a word or phrase appears three or more times (≥ 3)

 - PT and item name repetition 
 - Singular and Plurals 
 Words with accents (non-EN languages)
**What does not qualify to be Redundant?** 
 - Connecting words (articles, prepositions, conjunctions)
 - Synonyms of PT or item name or any attribute
 - Contextual repetitions

- **Ground Truth** - the verified, correct version of the modular title.

- Differentiator modified

Example i: Differentiator 'Bluetooth' could be more specific as 'Bluetooth 5.0' if version is known. 
- Example ii: Operating system Android 9 Pie instead Android (as referred from product description or bullet points)

- **Input:** ASIN, Country, Title, Bullet Points, Description sourced via CP Central or input datasets 
- **Processing:**

 Final audit decisions, observations and Ground Truth (optional) recorded in system outputs

Outputs used for catalog quality improvements and Starfish model training pipelines

- Upon completion of audits, the workflow auto generates the verification batch

- **Verification Batch**
All items from **Audit Batch** move to **Verification Batch**.
- The verification sample size is 20% (10 ASINs) and follows random sampling technique.
- The batches are auto assigned by work allocation algorithms or manually assigned bt work allocators to Verifiers. 
- Follows single blind verification system, where verifier evaluates auditor decisions on takes verification decision as Error or No Error.
- If all verification decisons are-
**No errors** in all ASINs → Batch completes → **No Loop Closure required**.

Outcome:
**Agreement** → Error **retained** → End.
- **Disagreement** → Error **reversed** → End.

### 8.1  TQS (Title Quality Score) Guidelines

High-quality titles help customers find products and scan search results quickly, so titles must highlight essential product information while remaining clear and readable. Avoid lengthy or cluttered titles that reduce discoverability. To provide the best possible customer experience, all sellers, vendors, and category teams listing products across Amazon's worldwide stores follow these additional guidelines. *[Source: **Seller Central**]*

**SI. #**

**Guideline**

**Compliant Example**

**Non-Compliant Example**

**Explanation**

1

**Length**: Keep titles concise, ideally Titles "Item_name" must not exceed 75 characters (including spaces). item_differentiator includes up to (≤) 6 item highlights, each ≤ 20 characters

"Amazon Essentials Women's Fleece Jacket, Black, Medium"

"Amazon Essentials Super Comfortable Women's Winter Fleece Jacket with Pockets and Zipper Closure Perfect for Cold Weather and Outdoor Activities, Available in Black Color, Size Medium for Women"

75 or fewer characters is recommended because mobile screens truncate long titles.

2

**Redundancy**: Avoid repeating words or information

"Levi's Men's 501 Original Fit Jeans"

"Levi's Men's Jeans Men's 501 Original Fit Men's Denim Jeans"

Non-compliant example repeats "Men's" and "Jeans" unnecessarily

3

**Special Characters**: Avoid excessive or decorative use of symbols. Punctuation should be used where needed

"Brother Printer, HL-L2350DW Wireless Monochrome"

"Brother Printer★HL-L2350DW★Wireless Monochrome★BEST SELLER★"

Use only standard letters and numbers. Don't use non-language ASCII characters such as Æ, Š, Œ, Ÿ, or Ž. Titles can include necessary punctuation, like hyphens (-), forward slashes (/), commas (,), ampersands (&), and periods (.).

4

**Information order**: Brand name, Flavor/style, Product type, Key attribute, Color, Size/pack count, Model number

"Amazon Fresh Decaf Colombia Whole Bean Coffee, Medium Roast, 12 Ounce (Pack of 3)"

"Medium Roast Decaf Coffee 12oz Pack of 3 Colombia Whole Bean Amazon Fresh"

Order the words to reflect the most important product information upfront.

5

**Parent/Child variations**: Include size and color in child ASINs only and not in the title of the parent ASIN

Parent: "Amazon Essentials T-Shirt" Child: "Amazon Essentials T-Shirt, White, Medium"

Parent: "Amazon Essentials T-Shirt, Available in Multiple Colors and Sizes" Child: Same as parent

The detail page displays the title of the parent ASIN, and the title of the child ASIN will only appear once the ASIN is added to the customer’s cart. The child ASIN title will not appear on the product detail page.

6

**Numbers**: Use numerals instead of spelling out numbers

"2-Pack Cotton Towels, 24 x 48 inches"

"Two-Pack Cotton Towels, Twenty-Four x Forty-Eight inches"

Use numerals: "2" instead of "two".

7

**Subjective commentary**: Don't use subjective phrases

"Sony WH-1000XM4 Wireless Noise Canceling Headphones"

"Sony WH-1000XM4 HOT ITEM Wireless Noise Canceling Headphones - BEST SELLER!!!"

Subjective commentary examples are "Hot Item" or "Best seller".

8

**Capitalization**: Don't use ALL CAPS

"Nike Air Running Shoes with Cushioned Sole"

"NIKE AIR RUNNING SHOES" or "nike air running shoes"

Capitalize the first letter of each word, except for prepositions (in, on, over, with), conjunctions (and, or, for), or articles (the, a, an).

9

**Abbreviations**: Measurements can be abbreviated

"Samsung 55-inch 4K Smart TV, 55 in, 2023 Model"

"Samsung Fifty-Five inch 4K Smart Television, Fifty-Five inches, 2023 Model"

Measurements abbreviations examples: "cm", "oz", "in", and "kg".

10

**Model Numbers**: Include model numbers for certain categories

"Samsung Galaxy S23 Ultra, 256GB, Phantom Black, SM-S918U"

"Samsung Galaxy Phone, 256GB, Phantom Black"

Model numbers are recommended for inclusion in titles for certain categories.
