Question: explain error free text

**User:** explain error free text

**Assistant:** According to the SOP, "Error-free text" is one of the 7 parameters for checking the quality of A+ Content. This parameter refers to the presence or absence of grammatical errors, spelling errors, and other text-related issues in the content.

Specifically, the SOP states that "Error-free text" refers to the absence of the following defects:

1. Grammatical errors
2. Spelling errors
3. Punctuation, cap, spacing issues
4. Consecutive 8+ lines of text
5. Keyword stuffing (Keyword density >40% in any paragraph)

To achieve error-free text, the content should be reviewed carefully for any mistakes or issues in grammar, spelling, punctuation, and formatting.

**User:** remove grammatical error

**Assistant:** According to the SOP, a "Grammatical error" is one of the common defects that can be found in the text of A+ Content. The SOP states that a grammatical error is present when the text uses incorrect verb conjugation, tense, or sentence structure.

The SOP provides the following example of a grammatical error:

"Fish decor— with stable and wide bottom, it can placed smoothly and not easy to overturn. Should be Fish decor-- with a stable and wide bottom, it can be placed smoothly and is not easily overturned."

To remove a grammatical error, the text should be revised to correct the error and use proper grammar and sentence structure.

Here's an example of how the corrected text would look:

"Fish decor— with a stable and wide bottom, it can be placed smoothly and is not easily overturned."

In this corrected text, the grammatical error has been removed by changing the verb "can placed" to "can be placed" and adding the word "is" to correct the tense and sentence structure.
