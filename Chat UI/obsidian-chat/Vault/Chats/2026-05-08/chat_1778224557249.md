**Assistant:** This SOP (Standard Operating Procedure) outlines the guidelines for annotating A+ content, specifically focusing on quality rubrics to train a machine learning model called A+ Quality Score 2.0. The purpose of this annotation is to enable the model to accurately evaluate content and provide downstream treatments.

Here's a breakdown of the key points:

1. **Objective**: The main goal is to annotate A+ content to train the A+ Quality Score 2.0 model, which will be used for tiered CX treatment and customized recommendations.
2. **Version Table**: The SOP has undergone revisions, with the latest update on May 19, 2025.
3. **Review Instruction**: To ensure accuracy, 3-5 individuals will independently review the content without seeing each other's results.
4. **Checks**: The annotation process involves 7 parameters:
	* Error-free text
	* Defect-free image
	* Redundancy-free
	* Rich product feature
	* Effective module usage
	* Style consistency
	* Image authenticity
	* Text readability
5. **Text Review**: Specifically, reviewers will examine text quality, looking for defects such as grammatical errors, spelling errors, awkward language, and punctuation issues.
6. **Checks are detailed**: There are specific examples provided for each defect type, such as grammatical errors, incomplete sentences, translationese, wrong capitalization, and spelling errors.

The SOP aims to establish a consistent and accurate process for annotating A+ content, which will help train the A+ Quality Score 2.0 model to provide high-quality results.

Question: explain the error free text

**User:** explain the error free text

**Assistant:** According to the SOP, "Error-free text" is one of the 7 parameters to be checked during the annotation process (Section 4, Page 1). This parameter is further broken down into two sub-checks:

1. **Grammar and Spelling** (Section 4.1, Page 2):
	* Grammatical errors (e.g., incorrect verb tense, subject-verb agreement issues)
	* Spelling errors (e.g., incorrect spelling of words)
	* Awkward language (e.g., translationese, incomplete sentences)
	* Punctuation, capitalization, and spacing issues (e.g., missing or incorrect punctuation, inconsistent capitalization, or excessive spacing)
2. **Text scannability** (Section 4.1, Page 2):
	* Consecutive 8+ lines of text (e.g., a block of text without proper paragraph breaks)
	* Keyword stuffing (excessive use of keywords in a paragraph, exceeding 40% density)

The SOP provides examples of poor error-free text, including:

* Grammatical errors: "Fish decor— with stable and wide bottom, it can placed smoothly and not easy to overturn."
* Incomplete sentences: "Penguin pattern socks— made of , strong and durable material for long time use,christmas socks."
* Translationese: "St patricks day party favors: good to your friends, they will like it very much."
* Wrong capitalization: "Outdoor solar light: solar powered light — the lights are powered solar energy. no external electricity required."
* Spelling error: "Cheese Tools: It can scrape out fine pieces on the surface of hard cocol"

To ensure error-free text, annotators should review the content carefully and check for these types of errors. If any defects are found, the annotator should provide feedback with reasons and elaborate on any additional issues in column AD.
