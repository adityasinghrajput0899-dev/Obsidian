Normalization is a process of transforming data into a consistent format, often to reduce data redundancy, improve data quality, and enhance data analysis. It involves standardizing data values, formats, and structures to ensure consistency and accuracy across different systems, applications, or datasets.

In general, normalization can be applied to various types of data, such as:

1. Data cleansing: Removing errors, inconsistencies, or irrelevant data.
2. Data standardization: Converting data into a standardized format, such as date formats or currency symbols.
3. Data transformation: Converting data from one format to another, such as text to numerical values.
4. Data aggregation: Combining multiple data points into a single value or summary.

Normalization is commonly used in various fields, including:

1. Data science and analytics
2. Business intelligence and reporting
3. Database management and design
4. Data quality and governance
5. Machine learning and artificial intelligence
