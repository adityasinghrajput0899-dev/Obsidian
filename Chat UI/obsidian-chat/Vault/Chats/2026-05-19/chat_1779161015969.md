**Assistant:** **Normalization in Starfish**

Normalization is a critical process in Starfish, as it ensures that product attributes, values, and descriptions are consistent, accurate, and easily comparable. This process involves several key steps:

1. **Standardized Terminology**: Ensuring that product attributes, values, and descriptions use approved language conventions, reducing ambiguity and errors.
2. **Units of Measurement**: Standardizing units of measurement for attributes like weight, length, and volume, making it easier to compare products.
3. **Abbreviations and Acronyms**: Normalizing abbreviations and acronyms to ensure consistency and avoid confusion.
4. **Data Validation**: Verifying that product data meets specific criteria, such as valid values, ranges, and formats.
5. **Data Transformation**: Converting data from one format to another, such as converting text to a standardized format.

**Normalization Rules**

The Starfish Modular Title Quality Audits.md document outlines specific normalization rules for various product attributes, including:

1. **Color**: Normalizing color values to a standardized format, such as "Black" instead of "Blk".
2. **Material**: Standardizing material values, such as "Cotton" instead of "Cotton Blend".
3. **Size**: Normalizing size values, such as "Small" instead of "S".
4. **Weight**: Standardizing weight units, such as "kg" instead of "lbs".

**Benefits of Normalization**

Normalization offers several benefits, including:

1. **Improved Data Quality**: Ensuring that product data is accurate, consistent, and reliable.
2. **Increased Efficiency**: Automating data processing and reducing manual errors.
3. **Better Decision-Making**: Providing stakeholders with accurate and comparable data for informed decision-making.
4. **Enhanced Customer Experience**: Offering customers a seamless and consistent shopping experience.

**Normalization in Action**

In the Starfish system, normalization is applied to various product attributes, such as product titles, descriptions, and values. This ensures that product data is consistent, accurate, and easily comparable, making it easier for customers to find the products they need.
